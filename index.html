Banaras
Benaras is older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend and looks twice as old as all of them put together”. The land of Varanasi (Kashi) has been the ultimate pilgrimage spot for Hindus for ages.
Jaipur
Jaipur became known as “The Pink City” when, in 1876, Maharaja Ram Singh had most of the buildings painted pink—the color of hospitality—in preparation for a visit by Britain's Queen Victoria. Today, the city is known for its bazaars, forts, temples, palaces, and wildlife sanctuaries.
Mumbai
Mumbai, often called the "City of Dreams," is a vibrant and bustling metropolis on India's west coast, renowned for its iconic Gateway of India, bustling street life, Bollywood film industry, and the stunning Marine Drive coastline, making it a cultural and economic hub of the country.
Agra
Agra is a city offering a discovery of the beautiful era. Agra has a rich history, reflected in its numerous monuments dotted in and around the city. The earliest citation for Agra comes from the mythological era, where the epic Mahabharata refer Agra as 'Agravana' meaning paradise in Sanskrit.
Goa
Goa, India's smallest state, is a coastal paradise renowned for its stunning beaches, vibrant Portuguese architecture, delicious cuisine, and lively festive atmosphere, offering a unique blend of Indian and European culture
Kolkata
Kolkata, fondly called the "City of Joy," is a vibrant cultural hub in India, renowned for its rich history, literary legacy, delicious cuisine, and grand celebrations of Durga Puja, making it a captivating destination for anyone seeking to experience the true essence of Bengal.
